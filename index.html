LET'S EXPLORE OUR UNIVERSE

The universe is everything that exists, including all galaxies, stars, planets, matter and energy. It was created approximately 13.8 billion years ago in an event known as the Big Bang. Since then, it has been expanding and cooling. Matter clumps into galaxies, stars and planets, while energy fills empty space. The exact nature of dark energy and dark matter, which make up approximately 96% of the universe, remains unknown.

Milky Way

The Milky Way is a spiral galaxy that is home to approximately 100 billion stars, including the Sun and its solar system. It is found in the observable universe and is one of the largest and brightest galaxies. The Milky Way is known for its spiral shape and ring structure, and is composed of a flat disk of stars, gas and dust, a central halo and a spherical halo. Most of the stars are located in the flat disk, while dark matter is found in the central halo and spherical halo. The Milky Way is constantly expanding and evolving, and astronomers continue to study it to learn more about its history and future.

SOLAR SYSTEM

The solar system is a set of planets, satellites, comets, asteroids and other celestial bodies that revolve around the Sun. It consists of 8 planets: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune. In addition, there are several natural satellites, such as the Earth's Moon, and many smaller objects such as asteroids and comets.
